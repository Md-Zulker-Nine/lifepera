10 Cultural Mistakes Tourists Make in Japan (And How to Avoid Every One)

ZN
Zulker Nine · June 10, 2026 · 8 min read
🇯🇵

Japan is one of the most rewarding travel destinations on Earth — but also one where cultural missteps happen constantly, often without the traveler even realizing it. Japanese culture values harmony, respect, and social awareness at a level that most Western visitors simply aren't prepared for.

1. Tipping Anyone, Anywhere

This is perhaps the single most important rule. Do not tip in Japan. Not at restaurants, not at hotels, not in taxis, not at ryokan (traditional inns), not anywhere. Tipping is considered rude — it implies the person isn't being paid enough and can cause genuine embarrassment.

What to do instead: Express gratitude verbally. "Arigatou gozaimasu" said sincerely goes far further than any tip ever could.

2. Wearing Shoes Inside

Japan maintains a strict separation between outside (dirty) and inside (clean) spaces. Always remove your shoes when entering a home, many traditional restaurants, ryokan, some temples, and even certain shops.

3. Chopstick Mistakes at the Table

Two chopstick behaviors are particularly serious: sticking chopsticks upright in a bowl of rice and passing food chopstick-to-chopstick. Both mimic funeral rituals associated with Buddhist cremation rites.
